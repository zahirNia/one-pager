Why does it matter?

Water in agriculture World bank

Water is a critical input for agricultural production and plays an important role in food security.

Currently, agriculture accounts (on average) for 70% of all freshwater withdrawals globally with as much as 50% being wasted due to overwatering and inefficient systems.

However, future demand on water by all sectors will require as much as 25-40% of water to be re-allocated from lower to higher productivity and employment activities, particularly from agriculture due to its high share of water use.

Irrigated agriculture is, on average, at least twice as productive per unit of land as rainfed agriculture, thereby allowing for more production intensification and crop diversification.

Irrigated agriculture represents 20 percent of the total cultivated land and contributes 40 percent of the total food produced worldwide.

Our solution

Inter-sectoral water re-allocations and significant shifts of water away from agriculture will also need to be accompanied by improvements in water use efficiency and improvements in water delivery systems.

Surface irrigation technique is both the most ancient and the most widespread in the world (about 90% of the irrigated area). FAO.org

Business model

Our technology

  • Effectively reduce annual water consumption by up to 40%
  • Increases crop yields by 20% to 40%

The customer pays no initial cost for having the technology installed. We define a Baseline for:

  1. Water consumption
  2. Energy consumption
  3. Product yield

And we share the savings with the farmer. The net benefit (savings - cost of the maintenance) is then divided 50%-50%, and the equipments remains our assets.
